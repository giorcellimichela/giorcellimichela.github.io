About

I am an Assistant Professor of Economics at the University of California, Los Angeles, a Faculty Research Fellow at the NBER, and a Research Fellow at the CEPR. My research lies in economic history and applied microeconomics, with a focus on the development and diffusion of management and technology innovations and their impact on firm productivity.

My work draws on historical episodes — from the Marshall Plan's Productivity Program to technology transfer between the Soviet Union and China — to understand how firms learn to be more productive and how those gains persist. I received my Ph.D. in Economics from Stanford University in 2016.
